# Breakout Archetypes

Reusable bullish expansion patterns from the current Signal Grammar Lab.

These are hypotheses, not production signals. They should become measurable events and pass Layer 7 evidence before affecting ranking, states, or the TradingView indicator.

## Multi-Level Reclaim Confluence

The oscillator breaks structure and reclaims several important things close together:

- oscillator downtrend or wedge break
- viscosity reclaim
- `-2` or `-1.5` reclaim
- flattening or rising viscosity
- fast color/gradient improvement

## Lower-Zone Coil Then Reclaim

Signal compresses around the lower zones, usually between `-2` and `-1.5`, then weakness stops expanding and the line reclaims viscosity or a key level.

Representative concepts:

- [[Coil Under Viscosity]]
- [[Tight Coil Below Viscosity]]
- [[Lower Band Accumulation]]
- [[Relative Weakness Fails To Accelerate]]
- [[Weakness Exhaustion]]

## Viscosity Acceptance Flush Reclaim

Signal spends meaningful time above viscosity, briefly flushes below it, then quickly reclaims. This often distinguishes constructive compression from random chop.

Representative concepts:

- [[Time Above Viscosity]]
- [[Sustained Above Viscosity]]
- [[Failed Viscosity Breakdown]]
- [[Reclaim After Flush]]
- [[Viscosity Support]]

## Failed Breakout Reset Reclaim

The first structure break fails or needs more chop, but the second setup works after a reset, reclaim, and better level confirmation.

Representative concepts:

## Low In Before Price Confirmation

The oscillator forms a basing or trendline-break structure while price still needs time. The low may already be in, but price confirmation comes later.

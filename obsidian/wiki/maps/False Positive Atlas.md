# False Positive Atlas

Collect failed setup notes here. Failures are first-class research data.

These are avoid or caution archetypes from the current Signal Grammar Lab. They should become measurable filters or negative setup-quality features only after evidence supports them.

## Weak Viscosity Chop

The signal crosses above and below viscosity without clean structure, strong color shift, compression, or level confirmation.

Representative concepts:

- [[Weak Viscosity Chop]]
- [[Chop Around Viscosity]]
- [[No Compression]]
- [[Unstructured Volatility]]
- [[Weak Colors]]

## Steep Downtrend With No Meaningful Bounce

A steep oscillator downtrend breaks or flattens, but there is no real impulse, no clean reclaim, and no sustained pressure acceptance.

Representative concepts:

- [[Steep Oscillator Downtrend]]
- [[No Meaningful Bounce]]
- [[Weak Breakout Response]]
- [[Zero Line Not Reached]]
- [[Not Ready]]

## Failed Zero Confirmation

The oscillator improves from low zones but cannot reclaim or hold zero. Price may need more chop or may roll over.

Representative concepts:

- [[Failed Zero Reclaim]]
- [[Zero Confirmation Missing]]
- [[Zero Rejection]]
- [[Wedge Without Confirmation]]
- [[Price Needs Chop]]

## Hidden Bearish Or Color Divergence

Price pushes while oscillator momentum, color quality, or highs weaken. This can warn that apparent strength is losing force.

Representative concepts:

- [[Hidden Bearish Divergence]]
- [[Bearish Divergence]]
- [[Color Divergence]]
- [[Weaker Second Color Push]]
- [[Gradient Momentum Divergence]]

## Impulse Sold Off

Large oscillator spikes are immediately sold or retraced. The setup may need a second base or full reset before it becomes useful.

Representative concepts:

- [[Impulse Sold Off]]
- [[Failed Impulse Spike]]
- [[Spike Retrace Reset Needed]]
- [[Second Base Required]]
- [[Double Bottom Required]]
